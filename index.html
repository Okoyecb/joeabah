Dr Joe Abah(Ph.D)

Director General at Bureau of Public Service Reforms, The Presidency,Nigeria

About:

I attended University of Calabar from 1981-1985, hold a Bachelor of Laws, LL.B (Hons) degree and thereafter was called to the Bar by the Nigeria Law School, Lagos in 1986. I later proceeded to London Guildhall University, London, England where I obtained Master of Arts (M.A) in Business Law from 1990 – 1992.
In 2002, I was awarded Admission Certificate, as Solicitor by the Law Society of England and Wales. I proceeded further to Maastricht Graduate School of Governance, Maastricht University, Netherlands, where I am presently serving as a Visiting Lecturer and holds a Ph.D. in Governance with focus on “Governance and Public Policy Analysis”. I am a visiting Lecturer to the Institution.
I joined the Department For International Development (DFID) of U.K. in 2003 and served for ten years (four as National Programme Manager). Until my appointment as the Director-General of Bureau of Public Service Reforms (BPSR), I was the National Programme Coordinator for State Partnership for Accountability, Responsiveness and Capability (SPARC).

Publications:

Abah, J., (2012, Strong Organisations in Weak States, Boekenplan, Maastricht, Netherlands

It highlights the various constraints to good public service delivery in developing countries, but shows that, despite the daunting constraints, some organisations are still managing to deliver the outputs that they were set up to deliver. It also sets out the research question that the study seeks to answer, the dependent and independent variables used, the initial hypotheses advanced, and the contributions that the research will make to existing literature.
